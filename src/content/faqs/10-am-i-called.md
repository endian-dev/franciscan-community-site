---
question: Am I called to be a Secular Franciscan?
order: 100
published: true
---

After being with your local monthly fraternity meetings for about six months, you will have a feel for the Franciscan secular way of life and spirituality. You will begin to understand what St. Francis and St. Clare of Assisi have to tell us about following in the footsteps of Christ, praising God, and knowing that God's Holy Spirit dwells within us. You will begin to get a feel for Franciscan spirituality, care for God's creation, and learning to see others with Christ-like eyes.

Each of us is guided in our discernment process by God's Holy Spirit as we listen to the Gospel and the Psalms, to the writings of St. Francis and St. Clare of Assisi, to the Franciscan charism - our Rule - and to sisterhood and brotherhood in fraternity. This is the lived charism of Franciscan spirituality.

You will also get a feel for whether this suits you and your way of living. If it does, then you can formally ask to be admitted into formation. The first stage is Orientation and lasts for about three months, followed by Inquiry, which lasts about nine months. The final stage is Candidacy and is usually for two years. During Inquiry and Candidacy, there are a variety of learning modules to complete and guide you on your way to Profession.
